History Of PIMD

The Pan-African Institute of Municipal Development (PIMD) was established in 2020, during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, to support sustainable municipal governance in African countries through capacity building, leadership development, technical assistance, and knowledge transfer.

PIMD’s Board of Directors combines over 70 years of public sector and local government expertise in U.S. government agencies, including the International Finance Corporation, USAID, MCC, Department of Transportation, the State Department, and the District of Columbia’s Executive Office of the Mayor.

Our hands-on approach and practicum research tailored to local challenges provide tools and best practices to meet specific growth objectives. Our programs include robust professional development training curated to promote effective and sustainable municipal leadership. These objectives are achieved through training and research projects specially tailored to target municipalities and strengthen local capacity, develop a competent workforce, and transform municipal employees into powerful partners and allies in the development of the African continent.

Since our founding in 2020, we have partnered with Yale University to co-design the “How to Tackle Critical Challenges: A Simulation” for the Strategic Impact Leadership Forum hosted at Yale. We have also been invited to participate in the U.S. Africa Leadership Summit hosted by the Biden Administration in 2022 to share our local development and capacity-building model. In addition, PIMD has collaborated closely with the U.S. Embassy in Senegal to implement a Good Governance Practice Program (GGPP) in Guinea-Bissau, designed for civil society members, young professionals, and local government leaders.

2020

PIMD was established in Washington, DC in the height of the pandemic to support sustainable municipal development in African countries.

2021

PIMD Inaugural Board of Directors was created, at its helm, Excellency Omar Arouna, Former Ambassador of the Republic of Benin to the United States

2022

PIMD launched its inaugural Good Governance Executive Leadership (GGEL) and welcomed 20 Mayors and Senior government leaders from Senegal to Washington, DC for a 10-day peer-to-peer and practical training on good governance practice.

2022

During the U.S. Africa Summit hosted by the Biden-Harris Administration, PIMD was invited to join a breakout session to share its model for capacity building at the local level.

2023

PIMD implemented its first program on the African continent with the Good Governance Practice Program (GGPP) in Guinea-Bissau, in partnership with the U.S. Embassy in Senegal.

2023

In June 2023, PIMD joined Yale University and Mujeres por Africa for its Strategic Leadership Impact designed for senior African women leaders. The YALE-PIMD co-designed simulation exercise titled “How to Tackle Critical Challenges” uses case studies and real-life scenarios to stimulate critical thinking.
